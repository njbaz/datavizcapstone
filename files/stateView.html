The GYS Metric

The GYS metric stands for Grade Year Standardized and is a scale used to measure student achievement growth in terms of grade levels. It is based on standardized test data linked to the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP).

Estimation of Growth:

The average growth in test scores between 4th and 8th grades in 2019 is calculated. The score change between 4th and 8th grades is divided by 4 to estimate per-grade growth.

Scaling Data:

The data is scaled so that each unit of the GYS metric corresponds to 1 grade level of learning, referenced to the 2019 national population​ .

Interpretation:

A score of 0 in the GYS metric corresponds to the national average performance in 2019. Positive or negative values indicate whether students are above or below the 2019 grade-level standard. Purpose: The GYS metric allows for comparisons of academic performance across states, districts, and years, despite differences in tests and proficiency thresholds. It provides a unified measure of academic achievement recovery or decline post-pandemic​ .
