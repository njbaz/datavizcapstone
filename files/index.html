An exploratory platform for examining post-COVID Academic Recovery

An Example from California





About this Project

The COVID-19 pandemic reshaped education, causing significant disruptions in student performance, particularly in standardized testing. In California, these disruptions deepened existing disparities, with some students recovering more quickly than others. While tools like the Educational Opportunity Project provide valuable insights and lay significant groundwork, accessing and interpreting this data can be challenging.

This platform is designed to bridge that gap. It offers an interactive and context-focused experience, presenting both state and district-level data across California to highlight disparities in educational recovery. The goal is to empower educators, administrators, and policymakers with accessible tools to understand these trends and allocate resources effectively to support students in their ongoing recovery.



Who is this for?

  • Educators and Administrators: Explore recovery trajectories in your district to make informed decisions that support student success.

  • Policymakers: Access data-driven insights to design equitable policies addressing disparities in education recovery.

  • Researchers and Social Scientists: Investigate the long-term effects of the pandemic on student outcomes and demographic groups using synthesized data and visualizations.

What this platform offers:

  1. Visualizing Educational Recovery: See how student performance in math and literacy has evolved from 2019 to the present, with comparisons by subject, district size, resources, and diversity.

  2. Narrative Context: Gain deeper insights with stories that contextualize the data, uncovering trends, disparities, and potential causes.

  3. Interactive Exploration: Drill down into specific districts and demographic groups to explore the data most relevant to your interests.

  4. Informed Decision-Making: Access the tools you need to make data-driven decisions that support students still recovering from pandemic-related disruptions.
